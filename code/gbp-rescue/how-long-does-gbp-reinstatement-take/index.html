The honest answer

There is no official timeline

Google publishes no guaranteed SLA for reinstatement. Its “Fix suspended or disabled profiles” documentation says only that it will review the request and email you a decision — no timeframe is committed anywhere official. Any specific “3 to 5 business days” number you have seen is vendor-fabricated, not Google's.

What real-world 2026 reports actually show: roughly 3 business days for clean, well-documented cases, stretching to 4–8+ weeks for complex, escalated, or high-risk-category cases — and longer during active enforcement waves. In spring 2026 Google ran a series of AI-driven mass suspension sweeps; Sterling Sky logged a wave on May 5, 2026 tied to account restrictions, with industry reports of California high-risk service categories hit hardest in late April. During those windows, queues lengthen for everyone.

So the honest framing is a range, not a date. Anyone quoting you a firm “3 to 5 business days” is repeating a number Google never published. Plan around the worst case, not the best: assume weeks, not days, and make sure the one submission you file is correct so you are not restarting the clock with a denial. The variables that actually set your position in the queue are case complexity, your business category's risk level, whether the underlying violation was genuinely fixed before filing, and how busy enforcement is the week you submit.

Do not do this

The mistake that makes it slower

Filing duplicate appeals to “speed things up” does the opposite — it extends the wait and can get the listing flagged. Submit once, with the violation already fixed and matching documents attached, then wait. If you must follow up, use the official additional-review path only after a denial, never as a nudge.

How the appeals tool actually works

The reinstatement flow, step by step

The canonical tool is the Google Business Profile appeals tool (documented in Google support answer/4569145, “Fix suspended or disabled profiles,” and policy answer/13597551; colloquially the “reinstatement request form”). The flow is fixed, and most of the failures happen because owners rush a step:

  • 01
    Open the appeals tool and confirm the Google account.Use the account that actually owns or manages the suspended profile. The wrong account is a silent dead end.
  • 02
    Select the suspended profile and read the cited policy.The tool names a policy area. Read it — it tells you what the reviewer will look at, and what your evidence has to answer.
  • 03
    Submit Appeal, then click “Add evidence.”Submitting opens the evidence form. This is the moment the ~60-minute upload window starts.
  • 04
    Upload documents that match the profile exactly.Business name and address on every document must match the profile character-for-character. Submitting once with everything correct is the fastest realistic path — duplicate appeals only extend the wait.

The single biggest reason appeals fail is filing before the underlying violation is actually fixed — the reviewer sees the same problem and rejects again. The second is documents whose name or address don't match the profile exactly. Reviewers reject on inconsistency, not on story quality.
